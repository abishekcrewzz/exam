1) Problem Statement:

The EcoShop project addresses the need to connect eco-conscious consumers with environmentally friendly products and to promote green practices and sustainable living.

2) Objective:

The objective of EcoShop is to create a sustainable online marketplace. Its purpose is to allow eco-conscious consumers to easily discover, explore, and purchase environmentally friendly products, while also supporting local eco-vendors.

3) Goal:

The end goals and intended outcomes of EcoShop include providing a platform that successfully promotes green practices, supports local eco-vendors, and is designed to grow and evolve into a more dynamic marketplace in the future.

4) Result/Output:

The current and expected results include a functional website with:

  • An easy-to-use Navigation Menu.
  • A captivating Hero Section with JavaScript animations.
  • A Product Display Page (currently a placeholder for eco-products).
  • A simple Contact Form for user queries.
  • A Responsive Design ensuring mobile-friendliness.
  • Future functionalities planned include AI product suggestions, a carbon footprint calculator, vendor sustainability ratings, and a dedicated Vendor Portal.

5) Technology Used:

  • Frontend: HTML5, Tailwind CSS
  • Scripting: JavaScript (for basic animations and interactivity)
  • Icons: Flaticon
  • Deployment: Netlify
  • Future Expansion/Design Ready: React, MongoDB

6) User Audience:

  • Eco-conscious consumers looking for environmentally friendly products.
  • Local eco-vendors who want to list and sell their green products.

7) Impact on Society:

EcoShop aims to benefit society by promoting eco-conscious living and green practices. It connects individuals with verified sellers of environmentally friendly products, which helps foster sustainability and supports a more socially responsible approach to commerce. The project also contributes to the student's learning about clean design, modular coding, and integrating sustainability into web development.
